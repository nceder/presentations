What is Peer-to-Peer (P2P)..?

Peers of equal status (devices running appropriate software) cooperate in a loose decentralised network for mutual benefit. Peer-to-peer is the antithesis of hierarchy - where some have elevated status and power over others.

Peer-to-Peer (left) vs. Client/Server (right)

The client/server architecture of the web is a fundamental problem because the server always has power over the client.

The server is a single point of failure that is also the obvious target of attacks.

(Although sometimes hierarchy is good)

(Especially when command and control saves lives.)

In an ideal world, hierarchy is best when the obvious skill, knowledge and capabilities of an entity are acknowledged to bring about greater benefit for all.

In an ideal world, those with elevated status and authority would have earned it via reliable and consistent public displays of such skill, knowledge and capabilities.

In an ideal world, the responsibility and trust associated with status and authority would be a serious yet welcome obligation.

We do not live in an ideal world

Distributed Hash Tables

My current obsession as the drogulus.

  • A distributed and decentralised dictionary / data store;
  • No single point of failure or inadvertent point of control (e.g. no need for DNS);
  • Scales to huge numbers of nodes (redundancy++);
  • Relatively efficient;
  • Good handling of fluid network membership;
  • Solid foundation for infrastructure (resource discovery, key/value store, CDN etc...)
  • Tested in the real world (Bittorrent, Freenet & others).
